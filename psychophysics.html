Visual Psychophysics

Visual psychophysics studies the relationship between physical stimuli and human performance, bridging the gap between theory and laboratory experiments. This field employs specialized methods like Signal Detection Theory and Bayesian adaptive methods to characterize visual functions.

Judging Graphical Elements and Art Statistics

The accuracy with which graphical elements (e.g., bar charts, pie slices) are judged follows Stevens's law. Multivariate analyses show that 2D representations provide a 10% advantage in accuracy over 3D ones, as subjects must accommodate extraneous dimensions in 3D that do not carry meaningful variation.

In the context of aesthetics, image statistics such as edge density, luminance variation, and saturation variation can account for up to two-thirds of the variance in people's preferences for art. Participants are consistently faster at categorizing representational art than indeterminate or abstract art, likely because they use specific search strategies to find known objects in a scene.
