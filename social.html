Personality and Art Preferences

Personality traits and emotional factors are strong predictors of art-related behavior, style preferences, and aesthetic judgment.

Big Five Traits and Art Style Preferences

Predictable links exist between personality and specific art preferences:

  • Openness to Experience: Consistently the strongest correlate for art interest, knowledge, and engagement in aesthetic activities. Highly open individuals prefer surrealist, abstract, and novel art forms.
  • Extraversion: Positively associated with achievements in performing arts and preference for surrealist works.
  • Conservatism / Conscientiousness: Often associated with a preference for traditional and representational art rather than cubist or abstract styles.
  • Neuroticism: While artists are more likely to have higher neuroticism than scientists, the trait generally has a slightly negative effect on overall creative achievement.
