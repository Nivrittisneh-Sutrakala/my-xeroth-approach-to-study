The Poetic Repertoire of the Lucknow Gharana: A Categorized Compendium

The Lucknow Gharana, established by the Maharaj family, is renowned for its "Bhava-Pradhan" (expression-centric) style. The following list provides an exhaustive catalog of Kavits and rhythmic lyrical compositions, categorized by theme, with a primary focus on the Krishna-Leela repertoire.

I. Krishna Kavits: Natwar and Raas (Narrative & Technical)

These pieces depict Krishna as the "Supreme Dancer" (Natwar) and describe the beauty of the Raas-Leela at the Yamuna banks.

Yamuna Ke Tat Par
Lyrics: "Yamuna ke tat par Krishna Kanhaiya nirat karat sab gwal baal sang / Taathaiya taathaiya taathaiya tat ta..."
Krishna Kahe Murali Pe (Swarakavit)
Lyrics: "Dina dina cchina cchina pala pala gina gina / Taka taka driga driga tatatita krita krita / Tana mana ki sabh sudh abisraye viraha agina tana mana dhadhkaye... (sa re ga ma pa dha ni sa) Krishna kahe murali pe dhatittatit radha."
Cchamma Cchamma Cham Chananana
Lyrics: "Cchamma cchamma chama chana nana nanana / Nupur ki jhankar sunavata / Gvalabala sang Yamuna tata par / Sakhiyan ke sang raas rachavat... Shankh been dafa jhanjh mrichang bajavat."
Nirtatat Dhang Ang Sudhang
Lyrics: "Nirtatat dhang ang sudhang / Bhaye pavan mandh suganda sheetal / Bansi bata tata nikata jamuna / Vrindavan ki kunj galina mein radhe gopi umang."
Bega Aaye Do Darasha Murari
Lyrics: "Bega aaye do darasha murari / vyakul hain sab sakhiyan saari / Tum murlidhar adhar bajayo hum naache tum taan sunayo."

Bansi Ban Sri Krishna Adhar Lagi
Context: Focuses on the flute reaching Krishna's lips and the resulting divine sound.

Sur Bani Chahu Disha Baaje
Context: Describes the resonance of Krishna's dance music in all four directions.

Jo Hai Thakur Teeno Lokke
Theme: Krishna as the Lord of the three worlds dancing for his devotees.

Naachat Kanhaiya Yamuna Ke Tat Par
Context: A variation of the standard Yamuna Kavit focusing on the specific rhythmic patterns of "Naachat" (dancing).

II. Krishna Kavits: Chedchad and Bhava (Playful & Expressive)

These focus on "Chedchad" (playful teasing), "Makhan Chori" (butter stealing), or the "Khandita Nayika" (aggrieved heroine) themes common in Lucknow Abhinaya.

Kaanha Main Tose Haari
Lyrics: "Kaanha main tose haari / chhodo saari... suno bihaari nahin main doongi tohe gaari... nis din chhed karat nahin jaane de re."
Jaage Ho Kahin Rain
Lyrics: "Jaage ho kahi rain / alasane nain ratnare / chaal atapati ati aaye ho uthibhor / Maalinagaat, nakhlaagehai, kaapolanpe... Binda suno nahi ye maanat."
Mohe Panghat Pe Nandlal (Traditional Thumri-Kavit)
Lyrics: "Mohe panghat par Nandlaal gher liyo re / Mori naajuk kalaiyaa maror diyo re / Nipat jhapat mori sir ki gagariya phodi."
Jal Ki Naghat Abhare
Lyrics: "Jal kinaghatabhare / Mag kin napagadhare / Ghar kina kachukare, baithibharesaas... Kahe rasa nayak brijbani tana vidhabadhikakahaye."

Kahe Ko Mere Ghar Aaye Ho
Theme: A Khandita Nayika piece where Radha rejects Krishna's excuses after he returns home late.

Magan Suna Brij Shyam Lubhane
Lyrics: "Magan suna brij shyam lubhane / Dou hridya viraje... Radha who is also known as 'Sri' became the flute and touched his lips."

Charanan Gahi Pada Laagi
Theme: Surrender and devotion at Krishna's feet.

Mohe Chhedo Na
Context: A classic Lucknow thumri often presented with Kavit-style rhythmic interludes.

III. Ganesh Kavits (Invocatory)

Performed at the start to invoke Ganesha as the remover of obstacles ("Vighnaharta").

Shravan Sundar Naam Ganpati
Lyrics: "Shravan sundar naam ganpati gyaan nath gajaananam / Krit vighna haran vimochanam lambodar ekdant / Tata kar vandana liye pushpa dhoop aur deepta... tigda dig dig thei."
Gaiye Ganpati Jagvandan
Lyrics: "Gaiye Ganpati Jagvandan, Shankar suwan bhawani nandan / Siddhi sadan Gaj vadan vinayak / Kripa sindhu sundar sab layak."
Ganesh Paran (Teentaal)
Lyrics: "Gan gan ganpati, gajmukh mandal, git gin git gin git gin thun thun tat tat thei / Jay jag vandan vakratunda daani data vighnaharan."
IV. Shiva and Devi Kavits (Tandav Aspect)

Lucknow pieces that incorporate the vigorous Tandav elements through descriptions of Shiva or Durga.

Shesh Phan Dagmagyo
Lyrics: "Shesh phan dagmagyo... chalata shara, ladata bhara, katata binu munda bhaye, shrunita sarita chale."
Dama Dama Damru Baaje
Lyrics: "Dama dama dama dama damru baaje / bholenath shiva shambho bhaje... kashinath shiva shambho bhaje."
Joot Jata Mein Gangadhari
Lyrics: "Joot jata mein gangadhari trishool dhari damaru bajave."

Shiva Tandav Stotram (Rhythmic Adaptation)
Focus: Incorporating "Jata tavi galaj jala" into Teentaal rhythmic cycles.

V. Ram and Avatara Kavits (Bhakti/Devotional)

Compositions by Bindadin Maharaj focusing on the protective and liberating nature of Lord Ram.

Aiso Ram Hai Dukh Haran
Lyrics: "Aiso Ram hai dukh haran / Khech draupadi hai duhasan, cheer laago haran / Kheech haaro, cheer gadhyo."
Meri Suno Nath Jaise
Lyrics: "Meri suno nath jaise / Sabko dukh niwara / Ajameen ganika prahlaadko ubhara / Janak Janaki ki soch, dhanush tod daala / Gautam tiya bhaye phasaan, charan dharat taara."
VI. Seasonal and Specific Compositions

Pragate Brij Nandlal (Bhajan-Kavit) - Celebrating Krishna's birth.

Kanha Khelo Kaha Aise Hori (Hori) - Depicting the festival of colors.

Jhulta Radhe Navalkishor (Jhula) - The Monsoon swing festival.

Namaskar Ka Tukra / Salami / Rangmanch Pranam - A specialized technical piece used for the stage salutation.

Kaliya Mardan & Govardhan Lila - Epic-narrative Kavits depicting specific mythological events.
